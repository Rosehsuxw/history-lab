存储芯片泡沫与1987年幽灵 The Memory Chip Bubble and the Ghost of 1987

当存储芯片三巨头市值超越石油，你站在泡沫的边缘还是起点？ When the Big Three memory chip makers surpass oil giants in market cap — are you standing at the edge of a bubble, or the beginning of one?

📅 2026.07.16 ⏱️ 阅读约15分钟 ⏱️ 15 min read 🧪 含互动投票 🧪 Interactive Polls

2026年7月的第二周，全球资本市场见证了又一个历史性时刻：存储芯片三巨头——三星电子、SK海力士、美光科技——市值早已全部突破1万亿美元大关。DRAM现货价格在一个季度内暴涨50%，DDR5内存从450元涨至1800元，服务器内存单条从5000元飙至6万元。华尔街的分析师们高呼"存储芯片超级周期才刚开始"，而费城半导体指数却在7月15-16日连续两天暴跌超5%。 In the second week of July 2026, global capital markets witnessed yet another historic moment: the Big Three memory chipmakers — Samsung Electronics, SK Hynix, and Micron Technology — had all surpassed the $1 trillion market capitalization milestone. DRAM spot prices surged 50% in a single quarter; DDR5 memory jumped from ¥450 to ¥1,800, while a single server memory module rocketed from ¥5,000 to ¥60,000. Wall Street analysts were proclaiming "the memory chip super cycle has only just begun," even as the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index plunged over 5% for two consecutive days on July 15–16.

就在39年前——1987年10月，同样的狂热也曾席卷华尔街。那一年，道琼斯指数单日暴跌22.6%，史称"黑色星期一"。今天，当AI叙事主导全球资本市场，当"连小区保安都在问怎么买存储芯片基金"，我们是否正在重蹈1987年的覆辙？ Thirty-nine years earlier — in October 1987 — the same euphoria had swept Wall Street. That year, the Dow Jones Industrial Average plunged 22.6% in a single day, an event etched into history as "Black Monday." Today, as AI narratives dominate global capital markets and "even the neighborhood security guard is asking how to buy memory chip funds," are we about to repeat the mistakes of 1987?

决策风格诊断 Decision-Making Style Diagnosis

02 你是哪种决策者？ 02 What Kind of Decision-Maker Are You?

A选项 · 趋势信徒型 Option A · Trend Believer

你是市场的追随者，相信"顺势者昌"。你深知牛市是普通投资者最好的朋友，轻易下车只会错失财富机遇。你的投资逻辑是：基本面强劲+资金充裕+情绪乐观=牛市延续。在2026年的存储芯片行情中，你会重仓三星、SK海力士，相信"AI时代的存储芯片是新时代的石油"。这种风格能充分享受泡沫的超额收益，但缺点是往往在顶部加仓，成为"接盘侠"。 You are a follower of the market, believing that "the trend is your friend." You know well that bull markets are ordinary investors' best allies, and stepping off too early only means missing out on wealth. Your investment logic: strong fundamentals + abundant liquidity + optimistic sentiment = bull market continues. In the 2026 memory chip rally, you would go heavy on Samsung and SK Hynix, believing "memory chips in the AI era are the new oil." This style lets you fully enjoy the bubble's excess returns, but the downside is often adding positions at the top, becoming a "bag holder."

1987年选择A的人，在黑色星期一当天损失了30%-50%的资产。 Those who chose A in 1987 lost 30%–50% of their assets on Black Monday itself.

B选项 · 理性均衡型 Option B · Rational Balancer

你是有原则的现实主义者，深知"过犹不及"。你在贪婪与恐惧之间保持平衡，既不是死多头也不是死空头。你的投资哲学是"永远保留弹药"，无论市场涨跌，都不会all-in或空仓。这种风格的优点是稳健，缺点是平庸——在牛市中跑不赢激进派，但能在崩盘时保存实力。 You are a realist with principles, knowing well that "too much is as bad as too little." You maintain balance between greed and fear — neither a permabull nor a permabear. Your investment philosophy is "always keep powder dry"; whether the market rises or falls, you never go all-in or all-out. This style's strength is steadiness, its weakness is mediocrity — you can't beat the aggressors in a bull market, but you preserve strength when the crash comes.

1987年选择B的人，资产缩水20%-30%，但在见底时有足够资金抄底。 Those who chose B in 1987 saw assets shrink 20%–30%, but had enough capital to buy at the bottom.

C选项 · 逆向大师型 Option C · Contrarian Master

你是天生的怀疑主义者，相信"别人贪婪时我恐惧"。你熟读《乌合之众》，深谙群体心理的疯狂。"这次不一样"是历史上最昂贵的一句话——你对这种说法深恶痛绝。在2026年的存储芯片行情中，你会远离热门赛道，寻找被市场冷落的传统行业。 You are a born skeptic, believing "be fearful when others are greedy." You have thoroughly read The Crowd and understand the madness of mass psychology. "This time is different" is the most expensive phrase in history — you loathe such claims. In the 2026 memory chip rally, you would stay away from hot sectors and seek out traditional industries that the market has冷落.

1987年选择C的人，在崩盘后成为最大赢家——以"地板价"买入优质资产。 Those who chose C in 1987 became the biggest winners after the crash — buying quality assets at "floor prices."

D选项 · 对冲思维型 Option D · Hedging Mindset

你是金融工程的信徒，相信通过复杂工具可以规避风险。你精通期权、期货等衍生品，能够设计出"下跌有保护、上涨不缺席"的投资策略。没有免费午餐——高收益必然伴随高风险，但通过精妙的对冲设计，可以将风险降到最低。 You are a disciple of financial engineering, believing that sophisticated instruments can mitigate risk. You are proficient in options, futures, and other derivatives, capable of designing strategies that "protect against downside without missing upside." There is no free lunch — high returns inevitably come with high risk — but through elegant hedging, risk can be minimized.

1987年选择D的人，如果对冲比例得当，可以在崩盘中毫发无损甚至逆势盈利。 Those who chose D in 1987, if their hedge ratios were right, could emerge from the crash unscathed or even profit against the trend.

03 1987年黑色星期一 03 Black Monday 1987

"华尔街没有新事物，因为投机像山岳一样古老。" "There is nothing new on Wall Street, because speculation is as old as the hills."

—— 杰西·利弗莫尔 — Jesse Livermore

1987年10月19日，"黑色星期一"降临。 October 19, 1987 — "Black Monday" descended.

道琼斯指数单日暴跌508.32点，跌幅22.6%，创下史上最大单日跌幅纪录。全球市场同步崩盘： The Dow Jones Industrial Average plunged 508.32 points in a single day, a drop of 22.6% — the largest single-day decline in history. Markets worldwide collapsed in unison:

  • • 香港恒生指数暴跌33% • Hong Kong Hang Seng Index plunged 33%
  • • 伦敦金融时报100指数下跌10.8% • London FTSE 100 fell 10.8%
  • • 东京日经指数暴跌14.9% • Tokyo Nikkei plunged 14.9%
  • • 投资者一夜之间损失超过5000亿美元市值 • Investors lost over $500 billion in market value overnight

崩盘的原因是什么？ What caused the crash?

⚙️ 程序化交易的"死亡螺旋" ⚙️ The "Death Spiral" of Program Trading

组合保险策略在市场下跌时自动触发卖出，形成"下跌→卖单→更跌→更多卖单"的螺旋。当天超过40%的抛盘来自程序化交易。 Portfolio insurance strategies automatically triggered selling as markets declined, creating a spiral of "falling → sell orders → falling more → more sell orders." Over 40% of selling volume that day came from program trading.

📈 估值泡沫破裂 📈 Valuation Bubble Burst

1982-1987年美股连涨5年，道指涨250%。标普500市盈率从7.5倍飙至22.5倍，股息率从5.5%降至2.4%。市场严重透支未来增长。 U.S. stocks rose for 5 consecutive years from 1982–1987, with the DJIA gaining 250%. The S&P 500 P/E ratio surged from 7.5x to 22.5x; dividend yield fell from 5.5% to 2.4%. The market had severely透支 future growth.

🏦 美联储政策收紧预期 🏦 Fed Tightening Expectations

格林斯潘刚就任美联储主席24天就宣布加息，释放收紧流动性信号。市场对流动性收紧的担忧开始升温。 Greenspan, just 24 days into his tenure as Fed Chair, announced a rate hike, signaling a tightening of liquidity. Market concerns about liquidity tightening began to escalate.

💰 国际资金外流 💰 International Capital Outflows

美元持续走弱，贸易摩擦加剧，外资开始撤离美股市场——流动性虹吸效应的极致演绎。 The dollar continued to weaken, trade friction intensified, and foreign capital began fleeing U.S. equities — the ultimate manifestation of the "liquidity siphon" effect.

崩盘后，谁成为赢家？ After the crash, who emerged as winners?

保罗·都铎·琼斯：崩盘前一周果断减仓60%，成功躲过最惨烈的下跌。他后来回忆："我在暴跌前闻到了血腥味。" Paul Tudor Jones: Decisively reduced positions by 60% one week before the crash, successfully dodging the worst of the decline. He later recalled: "I smelled blood before the plunge."

大卫·德瑞曼（逆向投资之父）：崩盘当天告诉团队"这不是1929年"，重点买入花旗银行。1988年花旗反弹80%。 David Dreman (Father of Contrarian Investing): On the day of the crash, told his team "This is not 1929" and focused on buying Citibank. Citibank rebounded 80% in 1988.

沃伦·巴菲特：崩盘后说"我开始买入股票了"，这是他一生中最好的买入机会。 Warren Buffett: Said after the crash, "I started buying stocks" — it was the best buying opportunity of his life.

"格林斯潘看跌期权"的诞生 The Birth of the "Greenspan Put"

崩盘次日，格林斯潘声明："美联储承诺提供一切流动性。"他立即降息、注入流动性——成功稳住了市场，避免1929年悲剧重演。但也埋下隐患：投资者开始相信"只要跌得够惨，央行就会救市"——这一信念在2008年金融危机中得到最充分的验证。 The day after the crash, Greenspan issued a statement: "The Federal Reserve stands ready to provide full liquidity." He immediately cut rates and injected liquidity — successfully stabilizing the market and averting a repeat of the 1929 tragedy. But it also planted a dangerous seed: investors began to believe "if the crash is bad enough, the central bank will bail out the market" — a belief most fully validated during the 2008 financial crisis.

🌳 反事实路径树：如果历史走了另一条路？ 🌳 Counterfactual Path Tree: What If History Took a Different Turn?

历史从来不是必然的。1987年黑色星期一之所以没有演变成1929年大萧条，是因为三个关键的"分岔点"。如果它们走了另一条路，世界将完全不同。 History is never inevitable. The reason Black Monday 1987 did not become the Great Depression of 1929 lies in three critical "forking points." Had they taken a different path, the world would be entirely different.

①

如果格林斯潘没有立即注入流动性？ What if Greenspan hadn't injected liquidity immediately?

分岔点：1987年10月20日，美联储声明 Fork: October 20, 1987, Fed Statement

▼
✅ 真实路径 ✅ Actual Path

格林斯潘在崩盘次日即发表声明："美联储承诺提供一切流动性。"立即降息、注入流动性——市场在两周内企稳，S&P 500在6个月内完全收复失地。美国避免了一场系统性金融危机。 Greenspan issued a statement the day after the crash: "The Federal Reserve stands ready to provide full liquidity." He immediately cut rates and injected liquidity — the market stabilized within two weeks, and the S&P 500 fully recovered within six months. The U.S. avoided a systemic financial crisis.

⚠️ 假设路径 ⚠️ Hypothetical Path

如果格林斯潘犹豫不决（毕竟他刚就任仅24天，尚未建立信誉），市场可能重演1929-1933年的连锁崩溃：银行挤兑→信贷冻结→实体企业资金链断裂→大规模失业。估计GDP将萎缩15-20%，失业率飙升至25%以上。全球可能陷入第二次大萧条。 If Greenspan had hesitated (after all, he had been in office only 24 days and had not yet established credibility), the market might have replayed the cascading collapse of 1929–1933: bank runs → credit freeze → corporate liquidity crisis → mass unemployment. GDP would likely have contracted 15–20%, with unemployment surging above 25%. The world might have been plunged into a second Great Depression.

②

如果程序化交易在1987年不存在？ What if program trading hadn't existed in 1987?

分岔点：组合保险策略与"死亡螺旋" Fork: Portfolio Insurance Strategies and the "Death Spiral"

▼
✅ 真实路径 ✅ Actual Path

程序化交易是崩盘的"放大器"。组合保险策略在市场下跌时自动触发卖出，形成"下跌→卖单→更跌→更多卖单"的死亡螺旋。当天超过40%的抛盘来自算法交易。道指单日暴跌22.6%。 Program trading was the crash's "amplifier." Portfolio insurance strategies automatically triggered selling as markets declined, creating a death spiral of "falling → sell orders → falling more → more sell orders." Over 40% of selling volume that day came from algorithmic trading. The DJIA plunged 22.6% in a single day.

⚠️ 假设路径 ⚠️ Hypothetical Path

如果没有程序化交易，当天的抛压将主要由人类交易员执行。考虑到当时的市场情绪虽然恐慌但并非绝望（没有银行倒闭、没有经济衰退数据），跌幅可能只有8-10%——类似1989年的旧金山地震跌幅。不会有"死亡螺旋"，市场可能在1-2周内企稳。 Without program trading, the selling pressure that day would have been executed mainly by human traders. Given that market sentiment, while panicked, was not desperate (no bank failures, no recession data), the decline might have been only 8–10% — similar to the 1989 San Francisco earthquake drop. There would have been no "death spiral," and the market could have stabilized within 1–2 weeks.

③

如果1987年没有美元贬值+贸易摩擦？ What if there had been no dollar depreciation + trade friction in 1987?

分岔点：卢浮宫协议与国际贸易格局 Fork: The Louvre Accord and International Trade Dynamics

▼
✅ 真实路径 ✅ Actual Path

美元持续走弱+美日贸易摩擦加剧，外国投资者恐慌性抛售美股。1987年10月，外国投资者持有的美国国债被大规模抛售，美元汇率急跌。国际资金的"流动性虹吸"加速了崩盘。 A persistently weakening dollar + escalating U.S.–Japan trade friction triggered panic selling of U.S. equities by foreign investors. In October 1987, foreign holders of U.S. Treasuries engaged in mass selling, and the dollar plummeted. The "liquidity siphon" of international capital accelerated the crash.

⚠️ 假设路径 ⚠️ Hypothetical Path

如果美元保持稳定+贸易关系缓和，外资不会恐慌性外逃。美国财政部长贝克在崩盘前威胁对日本加征关税的言论，是压垮市场信心的最后一根稻草。如果卢浮宫协议成功维持汇率稳定，外资持股占比不会骤降，跌幅可能收窄至5-8%——只是一次普通的"市场调整"而非"黑色星期一"。 If the dollar had remained stable + trade relations eased, foreign capital would not have panicked and fled. U.S. Treasury Secretary Baker's pre-crash threat to impose tariffs on Japan was the final straw that broke market confidence. Had the Louvre Accord succeeded in maintaining exchange rate stability, foreign holdings ratios would not have plunged, and the decline might have been contained to 5–8% — just a routine "market correction" rather than "Black Monday."

1

"这次不一样"的时代叙事 "This Time Is Different" — The Era Narrative

1987年，市场相信"个人电脑革命让股票估值应该重新定价"。2026年，市场相信"AI革命让存储芯片成为新时代的石油"。DDR5涨300%，服务器内存涨12倍，分析师们高呼"超级周期才刚开始"。叙事在变，人性不变。 In 1987, the market believed "the PC revolution calls for stock revaluation." In 2026, the market believes "the AI revolution makes memory chips the new oil." DDR5 up 300%, server memory up 12x, analysts proclaiming "the super cycle has just begun." The narratives change; human nature does not.

2

流动性虹吸与结构性撕裂 The Liquidity Siphon and Structural Fragmentation

70%新增DRAM产能被AI服务器吃掉，消费级面临结构性短缺。就像1987年科技股吸走市场大部分流动性一样，存储芯片正在虹吸全球半导体资金。韩股外资创纪录流出——全球投资者年内累计净抛售韩国股票636亿美元。这不是价值发现，而是流动性驱动的泡沫。 70% of new DRAM capacity is consumed by AI servers; consumer-grade faces structural shortage. Just as tech stocks absorbed most market liquidity in 1987, memory chips are now siphoning global semiconductor capital. Record foreign outflows from Korean equities — global investors have net sold a cumulative $63.6 billion in Korean stocks year-to-date. This is not value discovery; it is a liquidity-driven bubble.

3

"18个月暴利倒计时" vs "短缺到2030年" "18-Month Supercycle Countdown" vs "Shortage Through 2030"

市场正在上演一场叙事战争：乐观派认为AI需求是刚性的，供给约束是硬性的，短缺将持续到2030年；悲观派指出2027年末新增产能集中释放，当前暴利周期只剩18个月。SK海力士265亿美元融资是"趁热圈钱"还是"未雨绸缪"？长鑫科技IPO是"国产替代"还是"产能过剩的前奏"？ The market is staging a narrative war: bulls argue AI demand is inelastic and supply constraints are rigid, with shortages lasting through 2030; bears point to a concentrated wave of new capacity coming online in late 2027, leaving only 18 months of the current supernormal profit cycle. Is SK Hynix's $26.5 billion fundraising "striking while the iron's hot" or "preparing for a rainy day"? Is CXMT's IPO "domestic substitution" or "the overture to overcapacity"?

4

消费级 vs HBM的结构性分化 Consumer vs HBM — The Structural Divergence

不同于1987年的"科技股同涨同跌"，2026年的存储芯片出现了前所未有的结构性分化：HBM（高带宽内存）因AI服务器需求暴增，供不应求；而消费级DDR5虽然也在涨，但驱动力是"产能被挤占"而非"需求爆发"。这种分化意味着：崩盘时不会是齐跌——HBM可能比消费级更抗跌，但也可能因为产能集中释放而更惨烈。 Unlike the "tech stocks rise and fall together" dynamic of 1987, 2026's memory chip market has seen unprecedented structural divergence: HBM (High Bandwidth Memory) faces surging demand from AI servers with supply unable to keep up; consumer DDR5 is also rising, but driven by "capacity crowding-out" rather than "demand explosion." This divergence means: a crash won't be uniform — HBM may prove more resilient than consumer chips, but could also suffer more severely if concentrated capacity releases hit.

5

估值仍有争议 Valuation Remains Debated

即使按成长股估值体系，三星预期PE约6-7倍，SK海力士约6-7倍，美光约10倍——依然低于费城半导体指数26倍的平均水平。但问题是：存储芯片本质上是周期性行业，以暴利期的盈利来算PE天然偏低。当周期反转、盈利暴跌时，"低PE"会瞬间变成"高PE"。这正是1987年的教训。 Even by growth stock valuation metrics, Samsung's forward P/E is around 6–7x, SK Hynix about 6–7x, Micron about 10x — still below the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index average of 26x. But the problem is: memory chips are fundamentally a cyclical industry, and P/E ratios calculated at peak-profit periods are inherently low. When the cycle turns and earnings collapse, "low P/E" instantly becomes "high P/E." That is precisely the lesson of 1987.

📌 实验室笔记 📌 Lab Notes

  1. 1. "这次不一样"是历史上最昂贵的一句话。1987年投资者相信个人电脑革命让科技股市盈率永远提高，2026年投资者相信AI革命让存储芯片成为"新时代石油"。历史不会简单重复，但人性从未改变。 1. "This time is different" is the most expensive phrase in history. In 1987, investors believed the PC revolution justified permanently higher tech P/E ratios; in 2026, investors believe the AI revolution makes memory chips "the new oil." History doesn't repeat simply, but human nature never changes.
  2. 2. 程序化交易是泡沫的放大器。1987年是组合保险策略，2026年是量化基金和被动投资。当费半指数7月15-16日连续暴跌时，有多少是"基本面恶化"，有多少是"算法触发止损"？ 2. Program trading amplifies bubbles. In 1987 it was portfolio insurance; in 2026 it is quant funds and passive investing. When the SOX plunged on July 15–16, how much was "fundamental deterioration" and how much was "algorithmic stop-losses triggered"?
  3. 3. 流动性虹吸效应是见顶信号。当70%的新增产能被AI服务器吃掉，当存储芯片吸走半导体市场大部分资金时，其他所有资产都在失血。 3. The liquidity siphon effect signals a market top. When 70% of new capacity is consumed by AI servers, when memory chips drain most semiconductor market capital, every other asset is bleeding.
  4. 4. 长协订单是双刃剑。3-5年的长协订单锁定了未来营收，让存储芯片比1987年科技股更具防御性。但当周期反转时，长协也可能变成"锁定亏损"的枷锁。 4. Long-term contracts are a double-edged sword. 3–5 year agreements lock in future revenue, making memory chips more defensive than 1987 tech stocks. But when the cycle turns, these contracts can become shackles of "locked-in losses."
  5. 5. 2027年末是潜在拐点。新增产能集中释放+暴利周期结束+机构预期反转——如果历史押韵，这将是"黑色星期一"式的时刻。但也许这一次，AI需求的持续增长会打破周期律？ 5. Late 2027 is a potential inflection point. Concentrated capacity releases + end of supernormal profits + institutional expectation reversal — if history rhymes, this will be a "Black Monday" moment. But perhaps this time, sustained AI demand will break the cyclical pattern?
  6. 6. 活着比什么都重要。1987年活下来的投资者，都是那些"永远保留弹药"的人。投资的第一要务不是赚钱，而是不亏钱。 6. Survival matters above all. The investors who survived 1987 were those who "always kept powder dry." The first duty of investing is not to make money, but to avoid losing it.

🧪 样本外检验 🧪 Out-of-Sample Test

"流动性驱动泡沫周期律"的2026复测 2026 Retest of the "Liquidity-Driven Bubble Cycle Law"

【实验原理】 [Experimental Principle]

我们从1987年"黑色星期一"的历史案例中，提炼出了"流动性驱动泡沫周期律"：当某一资产类别在极短时间内市值翻倍，且上涨主要由"机构被动配置"而非"基本面超预期"驱动时，往往意味着泡沫接近顶部。因为流动性驱动上涨的本质是"钱推起来的"，机构被动配置形成"正反馈循环"——涨越多→被动买越多→涨更多，直到流动性枯竭。这个规律在A股历史上多次验证：2007年"五朵金花"、2015年"互联网+"、2021年"茅指数"，都遵循这一规律。 From the historical case of "Black Monday" 1987, we distilled the "Liquidity-Driven Bubble Cycle Law": when an asset class doubles in market value in a very short time, and the rally is driven primarily by "passive institutional allocation" rather than "fundamentals beating expectations," it typically signals an approaching bubble top. The essence of liquidity-driven rallies is "money pushing it up" — passive institutional allocation creates a "positive feedback loop" — the more it rises → the more passive buying → the more it rises — until liquidity runs dry. This pattern has been validated repeatedly in A-share history: the "Five Golden Flowers" of 2007, "Internet+" of 2015, and the "Moutai Index" of 2021 all followed this pattern.
